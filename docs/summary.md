# Complete Supplement & Nutrition Optimization Summary

**Profile:** 40yo male, 6'3", 210 lbs, training 4x/week for 6 months, 6–9 hrs sleep, no smoking/drinking, 128+ oz water daily, 100–200g protein daily.

**Goals:** Muscle recovery, muscle gain, fat loss.

## Current Stack — Evaluation & Adjustments

### Creatine Monohydrate — 5g/day ✅ KEEP, NO CHANGES
- Textbook-perfect dose per ISSN position stand
- Most validated supplement in sports nutrition
- Take continuously, never cycle
- Take with any meal; timing is secondary to consistency

### Ashwagandha — 500mg/day ✅ KEEP, ADD CYCLING
- Well-dosed at 500mg (clinical range 300–600mg)
- Confirm it's KSM-66 or Sensoril standardized extract
- **Cycle: 8 weeks on / 2–4 weeks off** — emotional blunting is widely reported in community after 4–8 weeks continuous use
- Ashwagandha stimulates thyroid hormone production — get baseline thyroid panel (TSH, free T3, free T4) and recheck after 8 weeks
- Take in the evening with food for sleep benefits
- If you notice emotional flatness or lost motivation, start off-cycle early

### Acetyl-L-Carnitine (ALCAR) — 1g/day ✅ KEEP AS-IS
- 1g is in line with community practice (sweet spot is 1–1.5g/day)
- Clinical mega-dose recommendations (2g+) are based on obese/cardiac populations, not active lifters
- Forum users report diminishing or negative returns (mental cloudiness) above 3g
- **Take with carbs** (~30–40g) for insulin-mediated muscle uptake — a Clif Bar, oatmeal, toast, banana all work fine; don't take fasted
- Optional: pair with R-ALA (200–600mg) for the classic bodybuilding forum stack
- Take continuously, no cycling needed
- Consider adding LCLT (L-Carnitine L-Tartrate) separately if you want exercise-recovery-specific benefits — ALCAR is the nootropic form, LCLT is the recovery form

### L-Tyrosine — 500mg/day ✅ KEEP AS-IS
- 500mg is a standard daily dose per community consensus and Examine.com
- Clinical mega-doses (9–14g) are from military sleep deprivation studies — irrelevant for daily supplementation
- Manufacturer recommendations: 500–1,500mg/day; FDA GRAS at 250–1,000mg
- On training days with TL Stim-Free pre-workout (contains 600mg NALT), standalone morning dose becomes optional
- Take on empty stomach or with carbs only — protein meals blunt nootropic effects (amino acid competition)
- Value is training focus/motivation (dopamine precursor), not direct muscle building
- No cycling needed at these doses

### Omega-3 Fish Oil (Sports Research Double Strength) — DOUBLE DOSE
- Currently ~950mg combined EPA+DHA per softgel
- ISSN 2024 position stand supports ≥2,000mg for athletic recovery
- **Take 2 softgels daily** (~1,900mg EPA+DHA) to reach recovery-relevant range
- Take with fattiest meal for absorption
- Continuous use — benefits require 4–8 weeks to alter cell membrane composition

### BPC-157 (InfiniWell Delayed Release Oral) — LOWEST EVIDENCE-TO-COST RATIO
- Animal data on tissue repair is genuinely interesting (35 preclinical studies)
- **Zero completed human efficacy trials** — the only one (NCT02637284) was canceled
- Nearly all research from one lab group (University of Zagreb) with financial conflicts of interest
- InfiniWell formulation pairs SNAC absorption enhancer with delayed-release capsules — contradictory design (SNAC works in stomach, delayed-release bypasses stomach)
- FDA Category 2 (significant safety risks), WADA banned since 2022
- Community cycling protocol: 4–8 weeks targeted for specific injuries, not indefinite
- ~$80–120/month with speculative oral bioavailability
- **Regulatory shift underway:** RFK Jr. announced Feb 2026 that ~14 of 19 Category 2 peptides expected to return to Category 1, enabling legal compounding pharmacy access with prescription — injectable route would bypass oral bioavailability issue entirely
- **Recommendation:** If peptide recovery is a priority, wait for reclassification and get injectable BPC-157 (possibly stacked with TB-500) through a licensed compounding pharmacy with a prescription. Redirect current ~$100/month toward proven additions below.

### Nutricost Pre SF (Stim-Free Pre-Workout) — REPLACE
- Citrulline at 6g is adequate
- **Beta-alanine at 1.2g is severely underdosed** — ISSN minimum is 3.2g/day, and it needs daily dosing (not just workout days) since benefit is cumulative. At 1.2g only 4x/week, zero carnosine accumulation
- Other ingredients (betaine, taurine, alpha-GPC) likely sub-clinical doses
- **Replace with Transparent Labs Stim-Free Pre-Workout** (see below)

## Protein Strategy

### The Problem
- 100g days (~1.05 g/kg) are sedentary-level and the single biggest limiter in the stack
- ISSN recommends 1.4–2.0 g/kg/day for resistance training → 134–191g daily at 210 lbs
- At 40, anabolic resistance requires higher leucine threshold (~3g/meal vs ~2g for younger adults)

### The Fix
- **Never below 150g. Target 170–200g daily.**
- Distribute across 4–5 meals of 35–45g each
- The "anabolic window" is largely debunked — total daily intake matters far more than precise timing
- Pre-sleep casein (30–40g) is an easy consistent addition

### Whey vs Plant Protein
- **Whey isolate is the clear winner** for post-workout: higher leucine (~10–11% vs ~7–8% for pea), PDCAAS/DIAAS of 1.0, faster digestion
- Plant protein only makes sense for dairy intolerance, vegan choice, or environmental preference
- Pea protein requires ~40–45g to hit the same leucine threshold as ~30g whey

### Current Proteins on Hand (Finish, Don't Repurchase)
- **Thorne Plant Protein** (chocolate): 22g protein/2 scoops, 140 cal, clean label (pea + chia + rice blend with MCT, monk fruit). Better of the two — use first
- **Orgain Organic Protein + 50 Superfoods**: 21g protein/2 scoops, 160 cal, but 18g carbs and 9g fiber. "50 superfoods" adds bulk without meaningful doses. Use for non-training days or morning smoothies, not post-workout
- Once finished, don't repurchase either — switch to TL Whey Isolate

## Bloodwork — Get Before Further Optimization

### Hormonal
Total/free testosterone, SHBG, estradiol, LH, FSH, DHEA-S, morning cortisol, full thyroid panel (TSH, free T3, free T4 — especially with ashwagandha), IGF-1

### Metabolic
Fasting glucose, HbA1c, fasting insulin, lipid panel, hs-CRP

### Micronutrients
25-hydroxyvitamin D, RBC magnesium (not serum — unreliable), serum zinc, ferritin, B12

**Test morning (7–10 AM), fasted. Retest in 3–6 months after implementing changes.** This data determines whether additions like tongkat ali are warranted or whether vitamin D dose needs adjustment.

## Notes on Influencer-Independent Evidence

- **Rock solid regardless of any influencer:** Creatine, omega-3, vitamin D, magnesium, zinc, protein optimization — decades of research, ISSN position stands, hundreds of RCTs
- **Strong independent evidence, but popularity amplified by Huberman/podcast ecosystem:** Ashwagandha, L-Tyrosine, ALCAR — all have pre-podcast research bases
- **Genuinely weak evidence regardless of source:** Oral BPC-157 — animal studies from one lab, zero completed human trials, questionable oral delivery
- **Momentous pricing reflects Huberman partnership** — products are quality, but comparable brands (Transparent Labs, Legion) offer similar sourcing/testing at lower price points
